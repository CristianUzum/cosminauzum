Difference between butterfly and moths

Like all other insects, butterflies have six legs and three main body parts: head, thorax (chest or mid section) and abdomen (tail end). They also have two antennae and an exoskeleton. The difference between a butterfly and a moth? Both butterflies and moths belong to the same insect group called Lepidoptera.

Butterflies usually have clubbed antennae but moths have fuzzy or feathery antennae. Butterflies normally are active during the daytime while most moths are active at night

attractions for butterflies

The number one step to attract butterflies is simply providing the food they like – which is generally plants.

Butterflies need heat to fly and they use the sun to warm themselves. If you see a butterfly just “resting” with its wings open toward the sun, it is almost certainly basking in the warmth. You could place basking stones in your garden for the butterflies.

The adult butterflies are attracted to nectar plants from which they sip nectar. They are also attracted to their host plants which are the specific plants where the females lay their eggs.

Don’t throw out your over-ripe fruit! While most butterflies prefer nectar from flowers, there are some butterflies that prefer nectaring from rotting fruit.
